Why ERGen Exists

As a BCA student, documentation has always been a significant part of my project work. Creating various diagrams like DFDs and ERDs is time-consuming yet essential. The necessity to switch between multiple tools for different diagram types — using draw.io for ER diagrams and other applications for remaining diagrams — creates unnecessary friction in the workflow.

ERGen was born out of this frustration. It's designed specifically to help fellow students create academically acceptable ER diagrams with ease, streamlining the documentation process and allowing more focus on actual project development.

Why Choose ERGen Over Other Tools

Before developing ERGen, I extensively researched existing solutions, each with their own limitations for academic use:

  • Mermaid — While excellent for quick diagram generation, it only produces ER diagrams in tabular format, which doesn't meet academic standards and requirements.
  • PlantUML — Though it generates structurally appropriate ER diagrams, the aesthetic quality often falls short of what external examiners expect in academic presentations.

Pro Tip: You can ask ChatGPT to generate Mermaid ER diagram code for your system, then paste that code directly into ERGen. Our tool will transform it into an academically appropriate diagram format with proper aesthetics.
